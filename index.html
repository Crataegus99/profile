About Me

Hello There!

My name is Samuel but you can just call me Sam.

I am a final-year engineering student who has recently completed my degree and am currently awaiting graduation from Swinburne Sarawak. My recent work focuses on embedded systems, FPGA design, and computer vision in resource-constrained environments, where I aim to develop strong system-level thinking and low-level optimization skills at the intersection of hardware and software.

Through my final year project, I gained hands-on experience designing RISC-V–based SoC architectures on FPGA, implementing classical computer vision algorithms, and applying hardware–software co-design principles to achieve real-time performance without relying on GPUs. While I still consider myself early in my learning journey, I have developed practical experience using Vivado IDE, VHDL, C/C++, and working with open-source processor cores such as NEORV32.

I am a curious and self-driven learner who works well both independently and in collaborative environments. I enjoy breaking down complex technical challenges into structured, testable solutions. I am currently seeking opportunities to further develop my skills in embedded systems, digital hardware design, and edge AI technologies.
